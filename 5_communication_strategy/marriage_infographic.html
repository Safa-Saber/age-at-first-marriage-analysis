Age at First Marriage in Asia

A Global Comparative Analysis of Marriage Patterns

Research Question

"How does age at first marriage in Asia compare to global patterns, and what do gender differences reveal about regional diversity?"

This study explores marriage age patterns across 192 countries, examining how Asia's diverse nations - from traditional societies to ultra-modern economies - compare with other continents. Using descriptive statistics and data visualization, the research identifies key trends in marriage timing, gender gaps, and regional variations that reflect broader social and economic factors.

üí° Main Insight

Asia demonstrates remarkable diversity: The continent contains both some of the world's youngest marriage ages (Nepal, Laos at 21.9 years) and oldest (South Korea at 38 years), reflecting a complex mix of traditional values and modern development patterns. Asia ranks 3rd globally (27.20 years), with marriage ages later than Africa and Oceania but earlier than Europe and the Americas. This diversity highlights how economic development, education access, and cultural factors shape marriage timing across the region.

Research Methodology

  • Research Approach: Descriptive statistical analysis with data visualization
  • Data Source: Wikipedia - List of Countries by Age at First Marriage (web-scraped)
  • Coverage: 192 countries across 5 continents (Asia: 48 countries)
  • Time Period: Data collected between 1980-2024 (56.25% of Asia post-2018)
  • Statistical Methods: Mean, median, mode, standard deviation, variance calculations
  • Analysis Focus: Continental comparisons, gender gap analysis, regional patterns
  • Tools Used: Python (pandas, matplotlib, seaborn, BeautifulSoup)
