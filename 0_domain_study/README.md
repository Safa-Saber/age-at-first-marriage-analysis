# Domain Study: Age at First Marriage in Asia

## Overview

This folder contains background research, domain knowledge, and foundational understanding of marriage age patterns globally and specifically in Asia. These resources provide context for why this research matters and what existing knowledge exists in this area.

---

## Why Marriage Age Matters

Marriage age is a critical indicator of:
- **Gender Equality**: Earlier marriage often correlates with lower women's rights
- **Education Access**: Delayed marriage allows more schooling, especially for women
- **Economic Development**: Wealthier nations tend to have later marriage ages
- **Public Health**: Early marriage increases teenage pregnancy risks
- **Social Progress**: Marriage age reflects broader societal modernization

---

## Key Research Questions in This Domain

Previous research explores these important questions:

1. **What factors drive differences in marriage age across countries?**
   - Economic development
   - Education systems
   - Cultural and religious norms
   - Gender equality policies
   - Legal frameworks

2. **How does marriage age correlate with development indicators?**
   - GDP per capita
   - Female literacy rates
   - Women in workforce participation
   - Gender equality indices

3. **What are regional trends in marriage age?**
   - Are countries converging toward similar ages?
   - Is there a global trend toward delayed marriage?
   - How do developing regions compare to developed ones?

4. **How does gender gap in marriage vary by culture and economy?**
   - Why do some countries have 1-year gaps while others have 5+ years?
   - What does the gap reveal about gender dynamics?

**Note**: My analysis describes patterns but does not answer these causal questions. See "Scope & Limitations of This Project" section below.

### Key Concepts

#### Marriage Age Definitions
- **Age at First Marriage**: Official age when person legally marries
- **Median Marriage Age**: Middle value when ages ordered
- **Mean Marriage Age**: Average across population
- **Gender Gap**: Difference between men's and women's average marriage ages

#### Related Indicators
- **Child Marriage**: Marriage before age 18 (linked to education dropout)
- **Early Marriage**: Marriage before age 20-25 (varies by definition)
- **Delayed Marriage**: Trend toward later marriage in developed nations

#### Socioeconomic Factors
- Education level correlates strongly with marriage age
- Women's workforce participation delays marriage
- Economic security needed for marriage in wealthy nations
- Cultural/religious norms influence timing
- Legal minimum age (varies globally: 16-18+)

## Global Patterns We Know

### Developed Nations (Europe, North America)
- Average marriage age: 30-35 years
- High gender parity in age (small gender gaps)
- Trend: Continuous delay since 1970s
- Driven by: Education, career focus, economic independence

### Developing Nations (Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia)
- Average marriage age: 20-25 years
- Larger gender gaps (men 3-5 years older)
- More stable patterns over time
- Driven by: Economic necessity, cultural traditions, limited education access

### Transitioning Regions (East Asia, Latin America)
- Wide variation within region
- Rapid changes in recent decades
- Examples: South Korea (38 years), Mexico (25 years)
- Driven by: Rapid economic development, education expansion, changing values

## Asia-Specific Context

### Diversity of Asia
Asia is incredibly diverse, containing:
- The world's oldest marriage age (South Korea: 38)
- World's youngest (Nepal, Laos: 22)
- Traditional societies and ultra-modern economies
- Multiple religions and cultural systems
- Varied legal frameworks

### Key Asian Patterns (What We Know)
- **East Asia**: Delayed marriage due to education emphasis and economic development
- **South Asia**: Earlier marriage due to cultural traditions and economic constraints
- **Southeast Asia**: Mixed patterns reflecting varied development levels
- **Middle East**: Moderate ages reflecting cultural norms and varied development

### Why Study Asia?

1. **Largest Population**: ~4.7 billion people (60% of world)
2. **Rapid Transformation**: Many countries undergoing major development changes
3. **Policy Relevance**: Understanding trends helps inform education, health, gender policies
4. **Diverse Models**: Contains both early-marriage and late-marriage societies
5. **Data Available**: Good quality demographic data exists for analysis

## Terminology & Definitions

### Important Terms
- **Gender Gap**: Difference in average marriage age between men and women
- **Marriage Cohort**: Group of people who married in a specific time period
- **Median Age**: The middle value when all ages are ordered
- **Mean Age**: The average of all ages
- **Outliers**: Countries with unusually high or low marriage ages
- **Regional Patterns**: Similarities in marriage age within geographic regions

## Research Timeline & Context

### Historical Context
- **Pre-1950s**: Most societies practiced early marriage (15-20 years average)
- **1960s-1980s**: Developed nations began delaying marriage
- **1990s-2010s**: More developing nations began transition
- **2010s-Present**: Continued global trend toward delayed marriage

### Current Moment (2024-2025)
- Global average marriage age: ~25 years
- Gap between richest and poorest nations: 10+ years
- Fastest changes in Asia (East Asia rapidly modernizing)
- Child marriage still affects 250+ million globally
